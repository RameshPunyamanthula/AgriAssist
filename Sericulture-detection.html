Flacherie Disease Detected

Flacherie is a common bacterial or viral disease in silkworms, causing sluggishness, blackening, and eventual death. Good hygiene is crucial.

Silkworm Health Management & Prevention

Immediate Treatment:

  • Isolate affected worms immediately to prevent spread.
  • Disinfect rearing trays and equipment thoroughly.
  • Provide fresh, high-quality mulberry leaves.

Long-term Prevention:

  • Maintain optimal temperature and humidity in rearing house.
  • Ensure proper ventilation and avoid overcrowding.
  • Use disease-free layings (eggs) and disinfect regularly.
  • Feed only fresh, healthy mulberry leaves.

For severe outbreaks or specific diseases, consult a local sericulture expert or research institute.

Uzi Fly Infestation

Confidence: 90%

Uzi flies are parasitic pests that lay eggs on silkworm larvae, leading to significant crop loss. Early detection and control are vital.

Pest/Predator Management

Control Measures:

  • Use nylon nets to prevent fly entry into rearing houses.
  • Collect and destroy affected silkworms and cocoons.
  • Employ biological control agents if available and suitable.

Preventive Strategies:

  • Maintain strict hygiene and sanitation in rearing premises.
  • Install sticky traps or light traps for adult pests.
  • Regularly inspect mulberry gardens for pest presence.

Implement integrated pest management (IPM) strategies and consult experienced sericulturists for persistent issues.
