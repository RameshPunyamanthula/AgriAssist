White Spot Syndrome (Shrimp)

Confidence: 90%

White Spot Syndrome Virus (WSSV) is a highly contagious and lethal viral disease affecting shrimp, characterized by white spots on the exoskeleton.

Species Health Management & Prevention

Immediate Treatment:

  • Isolate infected individuals/tanks immediately.
  • Implement biosecurity measures to prevent spread.
  • Consider depopulation for severe outbreaks to protect other stock.

Long-term Prevention:

  • Source disease-free broodstock and juveniles.
  • Maintain optimal water quality parameters (pH, DO, ammonia).
  • Implement proper pond/tank preparation and disinfection.
  • Avoid overstocking and ensure balanced nutrition.

Consult an aquatic veterinarian or aquaculture specialist for accurate diagnosis and tailored treatment plans.

Harmful Algal Bloom (Cyanobacteria)

Confidence: 88%

Harmful algal blooms (HABs) caused by cyanobacteria can produce toxins detrimental to aquatic life and human health. They often appear as green or blue-green scum on the water surface.

Water Quality Management

Control Measures:

  • Aerate ponds to increase dissolved oxygen.
  • Partial water exchange to dilute toxins.
  • Use algaecides cautiously and as a last resort, following expert advice.

Preventive Strategies:

  • Control nutrient runoff into ponds.
  • Regular monitoring of water quality parameters.
  • Introduce beneficial bacteria or aquatic plants to balance ecosystem.

Regular water testing and consultation with aquaculture specialists are vital for maintaining optimal water quality.

Feed Recommendation

Recommended Feed:

For Tilapia fingerlings, a feed with 35% protein and 8% fat, fed 3 times a day, is generally recommended. Adjust based on growth rate and water temperature.

Always consult a nutritionist for precise feed formulations.
