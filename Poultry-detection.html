Avian Influenza (Bird Flu) is a highly contagious viral disease that can cause severe respiratory issues and high mortality in poultry flocks. Immediate action is critical.

Poultry Health Management & Prevention

Immediate Treatment:

  • Isolate sick birds and contact a veterinarian immediately.
  • Implement strict biosecurity measures to contain spread.
  • Follow local authority guidelines for disease control.

Long-term Prevention:

  • Regular vaccination programs for common diseases.
  • Maintain strict biosecurity (footbaths, restricted access).
  • Provide balanced nutrition and clean water.
  • Ensure proper ventilation and avoid overcrowding.

For severe outbreaks or specific diseases, consult a local poultry expert or veterinary diagnostic center.

Pest/Predator Analysis Result

Rodent Infestation Detected

Confidence: 90%

Rodents (rats, mice) can spread diseases, damage feed, and cause stress to birds. Effective rodent control is essential.

Pest/Predator Management

Control Measures:

  • Set traps or use baits (with caution) for rodents.
  • Apply approved insecticides/acaricides for mites/lice.
  • Install bird netting to prevent wild bird entry.

Preventive Strategies:

  • Maintain strict hygiene and sanitation in poultry sheds.
  • Seal entry points (cracks, holes) to prevent pest access.
  • Store feed in rodent-proof containers.

Implement integrated pest management (IPM) strategies and consult local poultry experts for persistent issues.
